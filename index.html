About Event

This upcoming 2.5-day hands-on workshop

Jointly organized by BioExcel and supported by National Competence Centres in Portugal, France, Spain, and Ireland, this workshop will offer a hybrid learning experience (both on-site and online). The workshop will focus on using BioExcel core codes like GROMACS, HADDOCK, PMX, BioBB, and AlphaFold, with an emphasis on practical sessions and expert guidance.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Molecular Dynamics Simulations:
    • - Focus on GROMACS, including membrane-protein simulations.
    • - Performing alchemical transformations with GROMACS.
  • Biomolecular Docking:
    • - Using HADDOCK for Antigen-Antibody and Protein-DNA modeling.
  • Free Energy Calculations:
    • - Advanced methods for amino acid mutations and more.
  • AlphaFold Tutorials:
    • - Practical sessions for both AlphaFold2 and AlphaFold3.
  • BioBB Workshop:
    • - Protein conformational transitions and automatic ligand parameterization.

The trainers, developers, and/or experts in the use of the software will provide guidance and support throughout the workshop.

Who is this course for?

The BioExcel school is intended for intermediate to advanced researchers (primarily PhD students and post-docs) who are using or planning to use biomolecular modeling and simulation in their everyday research.

Participants should have:

  • - Familiarity with Linux.
  • - Basic knowledge of molecular modelling software.

    How to apply?

    To apply you will need the following information:

    • -> Support letter from your supervisor or a senior co-worker explaining why you should be selected for this course (in word or pdf format)
    • -> Research Abstract (limit: 250 words – you will be able to present and discuss your research during the course)

    Once you have these ready, please complete the application process by midnight (CET) on Sunday 9 January. Incomplete applications will not be considered. All applicants will be informed of the status of their application (successful, waiting list, rejected) by November.

    Details

    Date: 27 - 29 November 2024

    Venue: Presencial course

    Application deadline: October

    Notification date: October

    Participation: Open application with a selection (30 applicants will be selected for the course)

    Registration fee: 50 Euros (to be paid after the selection process)
